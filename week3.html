Week 3

This week we learn how to use two manufacturing tools: a laser cutter and a 3D printer. 


          
Laser Cutter

          
We were introduced to a laser cutting machine at the beginning of this week. Laser cutting is a manufacturing process in which a narrow, focused laser beam is used to cut and etch materials into specified shapes, patterns, and forms established by a designer. Ohood, a previous Fab Academy student, gave us a quick overview of the laser machine and demonstrated how to operate it.

          
          
We were tasked with designing and lasercutting something. I choose to make a medal key. This is my first time using a laser cutter, so here are the design steps:

          
          
 1- I designed the medal key using CorelDrew software. I marked the elements that I want the machine to cut in red and the elements that I want to engrave in black.

3D printer 

          
During the remaining days of the week, we attended a 3D printing session led by Sara Alhadrami, a past Fab Academy student. She gave us a quick overview of the tests used to evaluate 3D printers and how to use them. 3D printing is a technique for generating three-dimensional objects layer by layer from a computer-generated design.

          
Group assignment 

          
 For the grop assignment, every student is required to choose one of the 3D printing tests.I was assigned to do the angle test. Here are the steps:

          

          
1-Open the test file with .stl extinsion in Ultimaker Cura software

          

         
              

          

          
2-Select the printer , We will use Ultimaker 3 extended.

                

         
              

          

          
3-We adjust the setting of the modal by clicking on marketplace.

             

         
              

          

          
4-click on slice and preview.

                 

         
              

          

          
5-Click on save to disk, which save the file in .gcode.

9- click confirm when the material chosen start extruding.

          
10-insert flash then click on print
